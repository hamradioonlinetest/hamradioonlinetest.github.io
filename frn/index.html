Step 1

WEARC completes the session

After the exam session is complete, the session data and manifest move through the VE/VEC process.

Step 2

ARRL VEC submits to FCC

Our exams are usually in the evening. The ARRL VEC typically submits the batch to the FCC on the next business day, excluding weekends and U.S. federal holidays.

Step 3

FCC review and payment

After the FCC receives the application, Redlight Review is typically completed the same business day. New applicants can pay after that status appears.

What to do next

Understanding your status

  • Not found yet: Your session may still be moving through normal post-exam processing.
  • New Application Received: The FCC appears to have received the application.
  • Redlight Review Completed: Look for the FCC payment email or log in to FCC CORES to check for a pending application fee.
  • Payment Confirmed or call sign assigned: No FCC payment action should be needed right now.

FAQ

Common FRN status questions

When should I expect to see FCC status?

Our sessions are usually in the evening. After the session is complete, the ARRL VEC typically submits batches to the FCC on the next business day, excluding weekends and U.S. federal holidays.

When do I pay the FCC fee?

For a new amateur radio license application, payment is normally made after the FCC receives the application and Redlight Review is completed. At that point, check your email from the FCC or log in to CORES.

Do upgrade candidates need to pay the FCC application fee?

Normal license upgrades from an existing Technician, General, or Amateur Extra license generally do not need the new-license FCC application fee reminder. This tool focuses on new Technician applicants who may still need to pay the FCC application fee.

What if my FRN is not found?

Confirm that you entered the correct 10-digit FRN. If your exam just ended, check again after normal post-exam processing. If the delay continues, email the WEARC VE team for help.

When should I contact FCC Licensing Support?

Once there is evidence that the FCC has received your application, such as New Application Received, you may contact the FCC Licensing Support Center for help.

FCC Licensing Support Center: 1-877-480-3201. They are available Monday–Friday, 8:00 AM to 6:00 PM Eastern Time, except federal holidays.

If your status shows that your license was granted, you usually do not need to call the FCC. Check your FCC ULS record and your email for confirmation.
