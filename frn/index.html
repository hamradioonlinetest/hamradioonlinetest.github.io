How the process normally works

From passed exam to FCC payment or call sign

1. WEARC completes the session

After the exam session is complete, the session data and manifest move through the VE/VEC process.

2. ARRL VEC submits to FCC

For evening sessions, this typically happens the next business day, excluding weekends and U.S. federal holidays.

3. FCC reviews and requests payment

Once the FCC reaches Redlight Review Completed, new applicants can pay the FCC application fee through CORES.

Important: If your status shows Redlight Review Completed, look for the FCC payment email or proactively log in to FCC CORES to check whether your FRN has a pending application fee.
FAQ

Common FRN status questions

When should I expect to see FCC status?

Our sessions are usually in the evening. The ARRL VEC typically receives the session information after the session and submits batches to the FCC on the next business day, excluding weekends and U.S. federal holidays.

When do I pay the FCC fee?

For a new amateur radio license application, payment is normally made after the FCC receives the application and Redlight Review is completed. At that point, check your email from the FCC or log in to CORES.

Do upgrade candidates need to pay the FCC application fee?

No special payment reminder is needed for normal license upgrades from an existing Technician, General, or Amateur Extra license. This tool focuses on new Technician applicants who may still need to pay the FCC application fee.

When should I contact FCC support?

Once there is evidence that the FCC has received your application, such as New Application Received, you may contact the FCC Licensing Support Center at 1-877-480-3201, Monday–Friday, 8:00 AM to 6:00 PM Eastern Time, except federal holidays.
